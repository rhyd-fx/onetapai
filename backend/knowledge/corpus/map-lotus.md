## Lotus is a three-site map built on rotation doors
Lotus has three sites (A, B, C) connected by two rotating doors and a destructible
door, plus silent-drop mechanics. Like Haven, defenders cannot stack all three
sites, so information and tempo win rounds. The rotating doors let attackers fake
one site and swing to another quickly — and let defenders rotate through the
middle — so timing and sound discipline (the doors and drops are loud) are
central to Lotus.

## Lotus site fundamentals and the doors
A Site is taken via A Main and Tree, defended from Rubble and Tree; smoke the long
angles and clear Rubble. C Site is entered through C Main/Waterfall and Mound;
control the rotating door to cut or enable rotations. B is the connective mid —
holding it enables splits and protects the flank. Post-plant, use the doors to
delay retakers and hold from off-site space rather than the open plant spots.

## Lotus recommended agents
Recon initiators (Sova, Fade, Gekko) are premium because three sites demand
constant information. Cypher and Killjoy lock a site and watch flanks through the
doors. A controller (Omen, Harbor, Viper) walls the wide sites and Mid. Duelists
should entry the least-defended site and use the doors to reset tempo.
