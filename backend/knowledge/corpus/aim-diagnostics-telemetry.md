## Reading the hit distribution (headshot / bodyshot / legshot)
The ratio of head, body, and leg shots is the fastest read on aim quality. A
bodyshot rate above ~55% together with a headshot rate under ~20% is the signature
of a crosshair-placement habit, not a reflex problem — the crosshair sits at chest
height when enemies appear. This is trainable in days because it is a positioning
habit. A meaningful legshot rate points to dragging the crosshair down while
moving or spraying uncontrolled. Prioritize raising the crosshair to head height
over "aim training" when the body/leg share is high.

## Diagnosing "crosshair too low" (high bodyshot, low headshot)
When bodyshots dominate and headshots are low, fix placement, not speed. Keep the
crosshair at the height an enemy's head will be — level with door frames and box
tops — and pre-aim the exact spot a defender holds before you peek, so the mouse
travels a shorter distance. Drill: a two-minute daily head-level bot walk in the
Range (walk the map holding the crosshair on bot head height without shooting),
plus a pre-aim audit of the five angles you peek most on your best map.

## Diagnosing zero-damage deaths (moving while shooting)
Dying in a round while dealing zero damage — especially above ~25% of your death
rounds — almost always means you fired while moving and your shots never landed.
Bullets are only accurate when your character is fully stopped. The fix is
counter-strafing: release your movement key and instantly tap the opposite key to
zero your velocity, then fire the instant you stop. Practice ADAD counter-strafe
with first-bullet-only accuracy in a Deathmatch or the Range until stopping before
the shot is automatic.

## Diagnosing range-dependent accuracy (over-flicking at distance)
If your headshot rate at long range is far below your close-range rate (roughly
less than half), you are over-flicking — overshooting small, distant targets. Slow
down at range: switch from sprays to first-bullet taps or short bursts, let the
crosshair settle, and prefer micro-adjustments over big flicks. Calibrate drills
to your eDPI: high-eDPI players should train small-target precision and wrist
micro-adjustments; low-eDPI players need committed arm flicks. Never spray
uncontrolled beyond ~20m.

## Diagnosing opening-duel win rate (entry impact)
Opening-duel win rate is the share of first engagements you win. Below ~45% you
are losing the fights that set the round's tempo — usually from dry-peeking without
a trade or info. The fix is not "aim more": take opening duels with a teammate
within trade distance behind you, use a flash or recon before the peek, or hold
for information instead of challenging blind. A negative first-kill/first-death
differential means you should peek less often and with more support, not harder.

## Spray control versus tapping
Match the technique to range. Under ~10m, full sprays and tracking win; at 10–20m,
use 3–5 round bursts with counter-strafing; beyond 20m, first-bullet taps while
stationary or micro-strafing. Most spray failures come from firing past ~6 bullets
without pulling down or from moving mid-spray: the first three bullets go straight,
then pull down for the next few, and after bullet eight the pattern scatters.
Practice the pull-down against a wall at 15m to memorize the distance.
