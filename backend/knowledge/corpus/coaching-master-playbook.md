# The Radiant Coaching & Tactical Mastery Playbook

This master playbook serves as the definitive reference manual for competitive Valorant, containing granular tactical frameworks, mechanical diagnostics, and strategic playbooks. It is designed to be cross-referenced by players, analyzed by AI coaching agents, and used as a benchmark for high-level competitive development.

---

## 1. Core Roles & Strategic Execution

### 1.1 Duelist Playbook: Space Creation & Entry Spacing
Duelists are the tip of the spear. Their primary objective is not simply chasing kills, but **taking space, breaking defender crosshair locks, and creating entry trade paths** for their team.

*   **Entry Spacing and Trade Timing:**
    *   *The Spacing Rule:* Duelists must path onto sites exactly 1 to 2 seconds after initiator crowd control (CC) or controller smokes land. Entry players must not run in dry.
    *   *Trade Paths:* Duelists pathing onto a site must commit to a single direction (e.g., left or right of entry) and clear the "close" corners first. Teammates must follow closely (within 3-5 meters) to trade the duelist immediately if they die.
*   **Tactical entry techniques:**
    *   *Visual Disruption:* Use movement abilities (e.g., Jett Dash, Miks Dash, Neon Slide) to cross defender sights instantly. Dash directly into smoke (Cloudburst) placed on-site. This splits defender focus between the choke point and the smoke on site.
    *   *Opening Duels:* When taking opening duels, avoid slow peeks. Use wide swings on pre-aimed angles or counter-strafe tightly to gather info first.

### 1.2 Sentinel Playbook: Anchor setups, Delay, & Flank Guarding
Sentinels are responsible for locking down map areas, delaying enemy executes, and protecting the team's flank from rotators.

*   **Anchoring and Choke Point Control:**
    *   *Passive vs. Active Holds:* Do not peek choke points early unless backed by teammate utility. Set up traps (e.g., Vyse Shears, Cypher Trapwires, Chamber Alarmbots) in hidden cubbies or behind doorways where they cannot be shot before triggering.
    *   *The Delay Rule:* Your goal when anchoring is not to get a 5k, but to **delay the enemy execution for 10-15 seconds**, allowing your teammates to rotate. Use slow fields, mollies, and walls to segment the attacking team.
*   **Rotation Management and Flank Security:**
    *   *Flank Traps:* Place alarmbots or tripwires at the entry points of your spawn lanes (e.g., A-Lobby, B-Main links). Ensure they are deep enough to catch flankers but close enough to notify the team before the flanker strikes.
    *   *Stage Retakes:* If a site is lost, Sentinel players must fall back and wait for the team. Do not attempt to hold a site alone into a 5-man push; coordinate your remaining utility to clear the site during retake.

### 1.3 Initiator Playbook: Information Gathering & Execute Staging
Initiators gather intelligence, flush out passive defenders, and flash team entries to set up duels.

*   **Recon Dart & Drone Pathing:**
    *   *Information Geometry:* Deploy scanning utility (e.g., Tejo Recon, Sova Dart, Fade Haunt) at high points on walls or behind corners to maximize the scan's line-of-sight. Do not throw scans into the same spot twice; defenders will pre-aim and break them.
    *   *Droning Choke Points:* Use drones (e.g., Owl Drone, Trailblazer) to clear close corners (like Ascent B-Main cubby, Bind Hookah) before teammates push. Call out enemy locations and pre-aim lines.
*   **Execute Timing and Pop Flashing:**
    *   *Pop Flashing:* Flash through walls or high overhead where the flash is invisible to defenders until it pops. This prevents defenders from turning away from the flash.
    *   *Trading Support:* Initiators must follow behind the duelist, using damaging utility (mollies/breach stuns) to force defenders off key angles (like U-Hall on Bind, Generator on Ascent).

### 1.4 Controller Playbook: Smokes, Rotational Blocks, & LoS Manipulation
Controllers manipulate the map's sightlines, denying defender vision during executes and blocking rotators during post-plants.

*   **Smoke Placement Rules:**
    *   *Flush Smokes:* Smokes must be placed flush with the choke point walls. If a smoke protrudes into a site, it creates "pockets" that allow defenders to flash out or hide around it safely.
    *   *Shallow vs. Deep Smokes:* Shallow smokes block direct visibility at the entrance. Deep smokes (smokes placed further back in rotation lanes) deny rotations and split defender positions.
*   **Vision Denial on Rotations:**
    *   *Retake Smokes:* When defending a plant, smoke off the main entry paths (e.g., A-Main, B-Main) to force attackers to push through blind.
    *   *One-Way Smokes:* Position smokes on ledge geometry (e.g., Ascent A-Main arch, Bind Hookah window) so they hang high, exposing the enemy's legs while keeping the defender hidden.

## 2. Agent-Specific Tactical Breakdowns

### 2.1 Jett Mastery (Duelist)
*   **Cloudburst Dash Execute:** Bind your smoke key to a comfortable hotkey. Throw a Cloudburst smoke onto the site generator or default box, then instantly activate Tailwind dash into it. While inside, use the radar to check enemy positions and call out targets to entry teammates.
*   **Operator Positioning:** Always place your active Tailwind dash before holding an angle. Hold high-risk, aggressive vertical angles (like Haven A-Box, Bind Truck) with the Operator, take a shot, and immediately dash to safety.

### 2.2 Chamber Mastery (Sentinel)
*   **Rendezvous Anchor Placement:** Place your teleport anchor behind solid cover (e.g., B-Main cubby, Ascent Garden). Set up an aggressive off-angle peek (e.g., Mid Link) within range. Take one shot with the Headhunter or Tour de Force, then instantly teleport. Do not re-peek the same angle; fall back and play retake.
*   **Headhunter Economy Control:** Treat the Headhunter as a pocket guardian. Buy 4-6 bullets on eco rounds instead of buying pistols, and hold long-range lines.

### 2.3 Vyse Mastery (Sentinel)
*   **Shear Trap Timing:** Position Shear traps out of direct sightlines inside choke points. When the first enemy crosses, activate the Shear trap to wall them off from their team, isolating them for a free kill.
*   **Slow & Flash Coordination:** Use slow zone traps to delay rushes, and pop your wall flash when enemies are caught in the slow zones to secure trades.

### 2.4 Tejo Mastery (Initiator)
*   **Recon scan Geometry:** Deploy recon darts at steep angles. Clear close corners with flashes, then send the dart deep into site to detect rotate pathing.
*   **Info executes:** Call out scanned targets on the mini-map to guide entry duelists.

### 2.5 Miks Mastery (Duelist)
*   **Movement Entry:** Use speed boosts to quickly cross open angles. Coordinate entry pathing with initiators to secure trade duels.

### 2.6 Veto Mastery (Sentinel)
*   **Trap networks:** Set up alarm networks at flank points. Play crossfires with teammates near active detection traps.

## 3. Map Strategy & Positional Layouts

### 3.1 Bind: Teleporters & Lane Control
Bind has no mid, which splits control between A-Short/Showers and B-Long/Hookah.
*   **A-Site Execute:** Smoke off Heaven and Lamps. Duelists entry A-Short, while support pushes Showers. Initiators scan back-site. If defender rotates block the push, rotate back using the teleporter from A-Short to B-Port.
*   **B-Site Hookah Defense:** Sentinels set up inside Hookah. Use slows or mollies when the enemy enters Hookah, and coordinate crosshairs with a teammate playing cubby or back-site.

### 3.2 Ascent: Mid Dominance & Door Mechanics
Ascent is defined by its wide Mid area and openable/closeable doors on both sites.
*   **Mid Control:** Mid is the most important area of the map. Attackers must smoke Mid Arch or Catwalk to split defenses. If Mid is won, execute a split push onto B through Market or A through Tree.
*   **Door Control:** Close site doors immediately during executes to cut off rotating defenders. Play defensively from Heaven or Garden if you lose site control early.

### 3.3 Haven: 3-Site Defensive Rotations
Three-site maps require flexible rotation setups and active lane defaults.
*   **C-Site Defense:** C-Long is a long-range Operator lane. Position a Sentinel trap in C-Garage link, and let your Operator hold C-Long from Back-Site or Platform. Rotate early if Garage is breached.
*   **A-Site Defense:** Position one player on A-Long and one in Sewers. Retake A-Site from Heaven and CT Link, utilizing pop flashes to clear corners.

## 4. Mechanical Aim Diagnostics & Aim Correction

### 4.1 Engagement Distance Profiling

| Range | Distance | Primary Technique | Movement Style | Key Weapons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Close** | < 10m | Full Spray / Tracking | Run & Gun (strafing) | Spectre, Judge, Phantom |
| **Medium** | 10 - 20m | 3-5 Round Bursts | Counter-Strafing (A-D tapping) | Vandal, Phantom, Sheriff |
| **Long** | > 20m | First-Bullet Taps / ADS | Micro-strafing / Stationary | Vandal, Operator, Guardian |

### 4.2 Diagnostic & Corrective Actions
*   **Low Headshot Percentage (HS% < 15%):**
    *   *The Cause:* Dragging down too early, aiming at chest level, or spraying at long range.
    *   *The Correction:* Train crosshair placement at head height using map geometry (e.g., box heights, lines on walls). Stop spraying; practice shooting only 1-2 bullet taps in Deathmatch.
*   **Spray Control Failures:**
    *   *The Cause:* Firing beyond 6 bullets without pulling down, or moving while spraying.
    *   *The Correction:* The first 3 bullets go straight; pull down slightly for the next 3-5 bullets. After bullet 8, spray pattern goes randomly left/right. Practice spraying against walls at 15m to memorize the pull-down distance.
*   **Counter-Strafing (A-D Tapping):**
    *   *The Technique:* Press A to run left. Let go of A and instantly tap D to stop your movement. Shoot your burst the millisecond your character stops. Press D to run right, then tap A to stop and shoot again. This invalidates your movement spread while keeping you dynamic.

## 5. Economy & Anti-Eco Management

### 5.1 Round Buy Matrix

| Buy Type | Team Bank | Loadout Guidelines | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Eco** | < 1,000 | Sheriff, Ghost, or Light Armor | Save money to buy next round. Hold close corners together to trade. |
| **Half Buy** | 1,500 - 2,500 | Light Armor + Spectre/Marshal | Keep bank above 3,900 for next round. Focus on catching rotating enemies. |
| **Force Buy** | 2,500 - 3,900 | Heavy/Light Armor + Bulldog/Guardian | Buy down to 0 when you must win the round (e.g. anti-matchpoint). |
| **Full Buy** | > 3,900 | Heavy Armor + Vandal/Phantom + Abilities | standard buy. Full execute/defense setups. |

### 5.2 Anti-Eco Playstyle (Preventing Eco-Throws)
*   **Spacing:** Do not take close-range fights against eco teams. Sheriff/Shorty are lethal at close range but useless at long range. Play long-range lines, keep your distance, and group up. Never dry-peek corners one-by-one.
*   **Weapon Control:** If you die, call out the weapon location instantly so a teammate can cover or retrieve it. Allowing an eco team to secure a Vandal/Phantom is the primary cause of eco-throws.

## 6. Mindset, Consistency, & Tilt Diagnostics

### 6.1 Understanding Combat Consistency
Inconsistency is caused by variance in decision-making, mechanical resets, and tilt.
*   **ACS Variance & Stability:** A high coefficient of variation (CV > 0.40) in your Average Combat Score indicates you are a high-risk player who relies on momentum or hot streaks. To stabilize your games, reduce high-risk solo pushes, and stick to standard trading routines with teammates.
*   **Improving Stability:** Play with a consistent default setup on defense. Do not change your position every round if you get bored; consistency comes from mastering the same angles and timings.

### 6.2 Resetting After Tilt and Clutch Scenarios
*   **Resetting Mechanics:** When tilted, force yourself to take deep breaths during the buy phase. Change your position to a more passive anchor line. Focus purely on holding a crosshair line instead of hunting for kills.
*   **Clutch Rules (1vX):** In clutch scenarios, isolate duels. Never fight multiple enemies simultaneously. Use cover to turn a 1v3 into three separate 1v1 duels. Listen closely to footprint audio, fake the spike defuse/plant to bait the enemy out of cover, and play the clock.
