## The four roles and what each is for
Every VALORANT comp balances four roles. Duelists take first contact, create
space, and convert openings into site control. Initiators gather information,
clear angles, and set up safe entries with flashes and recon. Controllers deny
vision with smokes and walls, shaping which fights happen and when. Sentinels lock
down sites, watch flanks, and delay executes. A standard comp runs roughly one
Controller, one or two Initiators, one Sentinel, and one Duelist, flexed to the
map. Play the role your team lacks rather than instalocking a duelist.

## Current roster by role (2026)
- **Duelists (8):** Jett, Phoenix, Reyna, Raze, Neon, Yoru, Iso, Waylay.
- **Initiators (7):** Sova, Breach, Skye, KAY/O, Fade, Gekko, Tejo.
- **Controllers (7):** Brimstone, Viper, Omen, Astra, Harbor, Clove, Miks.
- **Sentinels (7):** Sage, Cypher, Killjoy, Chamber, Deadlock, Vyse, Veto.

Use this mapping when reasoning about an opponent's kit or a teammate's role: a
"Duelist death" means you lost a fight to an entry agent; frequent deaths to
Initiators often mean you are caught by flashes/recon and should play off the
info, not against it.

## How to pick within your role
Pick for the map and your team's needs, not a tier list. Duelists: Jett and Neon
want space and Operators; Raze excels in tight sites; Reyna and Phoenix are
self-sufficient and beginner-friendly; Waylay is a strong current entry. Recon
initiators (Sova, Fade, Gekko) are premium on multi-site maps that demand info.
Controllers: Omen and Brimstone are reliable solo-smokers; Viper is best on large
open maps; Clove is a flexible hybrid that can smoke even after dying. Sentinels:
Killjoy and Cypher anchor and watch flanks; Sage walls buy time and contest space;
Vyse, Veto, and Deadlock offer trap-based lockdown.

## Reading the current meta without chasing win rates
Metas shift every patch, so treat specific win-rate numbers as noise and lean on
role identity, which is stable. When the meta buffs a class (for example,
initiator cooldowns or sentinel survivability), the practical takeaway is to lean
into that class's fundamentals — more info-gathering and enabled entries when
initiators are strong, more aggressive anchoring when sentinels are strong. The
right pick is the one that fills your comp's missing role and fits the map's size
and structure.
