## Haven's three sites stretch defenses thin
Haven is the only three-site map (A, B, C), so defenders cannot stack every site.
This makes defaults, information, and fast rotations decisive. Attackers should
spread map presence, gather info, and hit the site with the fewest rotators —
then use the long rotation distances to punish over-commitment. Defenders must
lean on utility and traps to delay, buying time for a two-or-three-player retake
rather than trying to hold a site 1v5.

## Haven site-by-site fundamentals
C-Long is a long-range Operator lane — hold it from Back-Site/Platform and place a
Sentinel trap on Garage link; rotate early if Garage breaks. A is entered via
A-Long and A-Short/Sewers; retake from Heaven and CT with pop flashes clearing
corners. B (Mid) is the connective tissue — controlling Mid/Window enables splits
to A or C and secures the flank. Post-plant, extend off-site and hold crossfires
because retakes funnel through known links.

## Haven recommended agents
Cypher is exceptional here — Spycam and Trapwires cover the corners and flank
routes that a three-site map exposes, turning sites into punishing zones. Sova and
Fade provide the recon to clear the many angles; a controller (Omen/Brimstone) is
needed to smoke the wide entries. Duelists should entry the site with the smallest
defensive stack.
