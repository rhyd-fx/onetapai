## Round types by loadout value
- **Eco (save):** spend little or nothing, bank credits for a future full buy.
  Buy a pistol and light shields at most; play for damage and a possible weapon
  steal, not for the round.
- **Half / force buy:** partial investment (SMGs, Spectre, light shields). Used
  when a loss would still leave you able to full-buy next round, or when you must
  contest to stop the enemy's snowball.
- **Full buy:** rifle plus full shields plus abilities. Only commit when you can
  afford it two rounds running, so a single lost round does not break your economy.

## Buy discipline
The most common ladder mistake is force-buying every round, which guarantees you
are perpetually under-gunned. Coordinate buys with your team: five coordinated
SMGs beat five scattered half-buys. When in doubt on a broken economy, full-save
together and full-buy together.

## Reading the enemy economy
Track what the enemy could afford. After they lose a full-buy round, they are
likely on an eco or force — play for map control and picks, and do not over-peek
into desperate aggressive plays.
