## What tilt looks like
Tilt is a measurable performance decay across a session, not just a feeling. Its
signatures: your average combat score drops match over match, the gaps between
your queues shrink (rage-queuing), your positioning gets more aggressive without
information, and comms turn to blame. Once two or three of these stack, your win
rate collapses regardless of your mechanical skill.

## Reset protocols
1. **The two-loss rule.** After two consecutive losses, take a mandatory five
   minutes away from the screen. Stand up, hydrate, breathe.
2. **Box breathing.** Inhale four seconds, hold four, exhale four, hold four.
   Three cycles resets your arousal level between rounds.
3. **Process over outcome.** Review your last three deaths for your own mistake —
   not a teammate's — and name the fix. This restores agency.
4. **Session cap.** Hard-cap ranked at five games. After that, switch to
   unrated, deathmatch, or aim training. Chasing losses deepens the hole.

## When to stop entirely
If your combat score has fallen more than ~20% from where you started the
session and the gaps between games keep shrinking, stop queuing for the day.
Playing tilted trains bad habits and tanks your rank.
