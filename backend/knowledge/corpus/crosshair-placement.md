## Why crosshair placement beats raw aim
A high body-shot percentage is rarely a "flick" problem — it is a crosshair
placement habit. If your crosshair sits at chest or waist height when an enemy
appears, even a perfect horizontal adjustment lands a body shot instead of a
head shot. Fixing placement converts the same reaction time into more kills.

## The three pillars
1. **Head height.** Keep the crosshair at the height an enemy's head will be,
   roughly at the level of doorframes and boxes you are pre-aiming. Most players
   drift the crosshair to the floor while moving.
2. **Pre-aim common angles.** Before you peek, place the crosshair where an
   enemy is most likely to be holding. This shrinks the distance your mouse must
   travel, so first-bullet accuracy goes up without faster reactions.
3. **Discipline while moving.** Hold head height even while rotating or repositioning.
   Dropping the crosshair to the ground during downtime is the most common leak.

## How to diagnose it from stats
A body-shot rate above ~55% combined with a head-shot rate under ~20% is the
signature of a crosshair-too-low habit. This is trainable in days, not months,
because it is a positioning habit rather than a fine-motor skill.

## Drills
- **Head-level bot walk:** in the Range, walk the map keeping the crosshair on
  bot head height without shooting. Two minutes daily.
- **Pre-aim audit:** on your most-played map, list the five angles you peek most
  and memorize the exact head-height dot for each.
