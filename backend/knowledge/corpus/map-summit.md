## Summit overview and why it plays differently
Summit is a three-lane, two-site map (A and B) added in the V26 Act 4 pool. It
is larger than Ascent or Sunset, with a wide, boxy Mid that links to both sites —
so it rewards map control and rotation reads over raw aim. Attacker spawn feeds
both A Lobby and B Lobby without an early commitment; defenders sit behind both
sites with CT connectors between them. Treating Summit like a generic three-lane
map is the most common mistake: its reactive walls and mid connectivity change
what "holding an angle" means from round to round.

## Summit reactive walls (the defining mechanic)
Three droppable walls sit on Summit — one on A, one on B, one in Mid. Each has a
physical button beside it. Shoot the button twice, or deal 125 damage to the wall
directly, and it drops permanently for the rest of the round. Two rules that
decide rounds: (1) anyone caught underneath a falling wall dies instantly — even
in invulnerable states like Yoru or Clove ultimates. (2) Dropping a wall is a
commitment, not a reflex. On attack, dropping a wall behind you after taking site
cuts a defender rotation path and simplifies the post-plant. On defense, dropping
one early funnels attackers into fewer entries — but it also locks your own
rotators out. Delay wall activations until you have information on where the hit
is coming from.

## Summit Mid control
Mid is the most important area — "Mid is a bloodbath." It splits into Mid
Fountain (the central hub), Mid Tiles, Mid Bottom, and Mid Bend, with Mid Window
offering a push for both sides. Teams that win Mid Fountain rotate to either site
faster than defenders can respond, and can split A or B instead of hitting Main
dry. Keep at least one defender watching Mid every round: losing Mid hands
attackers free split pressure and a flank route. For defenders, a mid-fed flank
is a third retake angle attackers struggle to cover.

## Summit site play and post-plants
Both sites are small — A is tighter than almost any site outside Fracture, B a
little roomier. Planting and sitting on site gets you crunched from multiple
angles on the retake. The fix is to extend and take space after the plant: Gym
(B side, with access to Tower and Drop) lets you crunch site from above; Caves (A
side) opens an awkward peek into A Link and onto site. Defenders want those exact
two spaces for their retake, so the post-plant is a fight over Gym and Caves.

## Summit recommended agents and setups
A safe, well-rounded ranked comp is Omen, Raze, Sova, Cypher, and Sage. Sentinels
shine here: Sage walls play around the reactive walls for tempo swings, and
Cypher's camera survives wall activations for persistent info. Sova and Skye
exploit the map's many vents for recon and flashes — Sova can even trigger a
reactive wall with a single Shock Dart. Jett is strong for contesting Mid or
running an Operator on defense; Raze is better for clearing Summit's tight sites.
