## Wide swing (the aggressive over-peek)
A wide swing means peeling far off the wall into open space, exposing yourself to
multiple angles at once. It commits you to the duel — good when you have a read,
a teammate trade, or a movement advantage, but suicidal into an unknown crossfire.
If you keep dying far from cover with little time on the clock, you are
over-swinging into pre-aimed crosshairs.

## Tight peek (shoulder peek the angle)
A tight peek hugs the corner: you expose only the single angle you intend to
challenge and can retreat behind the wall in one counter-strafe. This is the
default for trading and for taking map control without over-committing.

## Jiggle peek (bait and gather info)
A jiggle is a rapid in-and-out to bait a shot or reveal a holder without
committing. Use it to gather information before your team executes, then decide
whether to swing wide or hold.

## Fixing chronic wide-swinging
1. Default to tight peeks; only swing wide with a reason (trade, info, util).
2. Clear angles one at a time instead of exposing yourself to several at once.
3. Respect timing: if you die instantly on the peek, the enemy was pre-aimed —
   jiggle first to break their crosshair placement, then re-peek.
