## Reading session tilt probability
A session tilt score is built from three signals: a statistically significant ACS
decline across the session, shrinking gaps between matches (rage-queuing), and a
second-half collapse versus the first half. Above ~0.7 the read is high tilt —
stop queuing now, take a 30-minute break with physical movement, and return only
when calm. Around 0.4–0.7 is moderate: take a 10–15 minute break, play a
Deathmatch or Spike Rush to reset, then requeue. The worst mistake is queuing the
next ranked game immediately after a loss while the gaps between your matches keep
shrinking.

## Feast-or-famine consistency (ACS variance)
A high coefficient of variation in your Average Combat Score (CV above ~0.4, and
especially above 0.5) means you are a momentum player — some games you hard-carry,
others you go missing. That volatility comes from high-risk solo plays and
inconsistent routines, not from lacking skill. Stabilize by reducing solo pushes,
sticking to standard trade routines with teammates, and playing a consistent
default setup instead of changing your position every round out of boredom.
Consistency is built by mastering the same angles and timings.

## Resetting after tilt
When you feel tilt, slow down during the buy phase and take deliberate breaths.
Switch to a passive anchor line and set a single, simple goal — hold one crosshair
line at head height — instead of hunting for kills to "win it back." Reducing the
number of decisions per round lowers the error rate that tilt inflates. If your
session ACS trend is clearly declining game over game, that decline is the data
telling you to break the pattern, not to force one more queue.

## Clutch composure (1vX)
Clutches are lost to panic, not odds. Isolate the duels: use cover and the clock
to turn a 1v3 into three separate 1v1s, never challenge two angles at once, and
use sound — fake a plant or defuse to bait an over-eager enemy out of cover. Play
for the time and the information you have, and take each fight only when the
geometry is on your terms.
